Something about me

I was born in the countryside of Sussex (UK). Yet, I ended up growing up – with my seven siblings – amid the Renaissance's art and the green of Tuscany (Italy).

At the age of seventeen, I voluntarily joined and served the Navy; I graduated from the Academy with a specialisation in Radar and TLC ...

... to then serving thirty-two months on a ship, including six months in the Gulf War (Operation Desert Storm).

I served as an LDS missionary for fifteen months. Despite being the most successful missionary in the field (the only one to earn the Master Teacher title), I was sent home a bit earlier because I was seen as too unconventional.

After a couple of years of web design and programming autodidacticism, accompanied by a six months old child and an adventurous wife, I abandoned all material possessions to seek Computer Science education in the United States.

I organise and guide my colleagues in outdoor adventures. Adventures such as climbing to the peaks of the Dolomites and French Alps and backpacking across vulcanos and glaciers in Iceland undoubtedly boosted confidence, resilience, courage, problem-solving, and leadership skills.

To stay insane but physically and mentally healthy, I run regularly, hike, climb, and – at least once a year – backpack solo in the wilderness.

Against all the odds, I'm [still] married and a father of two.
