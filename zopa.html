Zopa Bank

Head of Experience Design

Zopa's bank is scheduled to launch in 2020 and will sit alongside its peer-to-peer lending business. Zopa plans to offer FSCS protected deposit accounts, credit cards, and a money management app which makes use of data made available by the introduction of Open Banking.

Building, scaling, and coaching a team of Product and Service Designers.

Define and execute the user experience vision, strategy and tactics, team's code of conduct, user-centred design direction and guidelines.

Liaising with designers, developers, product managers, and business leaders to implement new conceptual ideas while developing and scaling a Design System.

Collaborating with the brand team to help ensure consistent tone of voice and appropriate design across all digital customer channels.

Enriching an emerging technology and finance-based culture by injecting a more user-centred and problem-solving mindset and process.

Identifying and driving emerging digital initiatives associated with usability, accessibility and design.

Design Strategy

After a few weeks of listening and observing, it became clear to me the importance to define the meaning of design and what success will look like. And, I had to act speedily as the business was eager to gauge the impact of my role.

I immediately defined and informed the meaning, capability, and purpose of design to key senior stakeholders. Critical to my team success was to clarify that the scope of design is to help position Zopa to succeed.

How does design help Zopa get to the next level?

Here is when I needed to read the current maturity and business goals of Zopa to make the strategy relevant, justified, and focused on the goals which matter most.

As this is currently ongoing, I'm hesitant to share details. What I can tell is that I defined the design Mission: "We practice design to humanise finance and technology". I then set a distinct objective, a strategy, and straightforward and explicit tactics. More will be shared in the future.

Working during a lockdown

I embrace change, and I always share my love for it with my team. I believe "change" is a decisive agent for testing and shaping your character.

In March 2020, the Covid-19 pandemic forced many out of their jobs while the lucky ones had to work from home. For now, we are part of the lucky bunch. Acknowledging this fact helped us to be grateful and to stay focused on the challenges and tasks ahead of us. Luckily, we were able to welcome to the team and build rapport with our new hires before the lockdown. We are a group of terrific human beings (the first trait for any team's member) and being able to practice trust and working together adequately equipped us well to minimise disruption.

I'm looking forward to seeing how much the lockdown will change the design in Zopa and us as a team and individuals – including the chance of me being made redundant :-).

Design Thinking

Design Thinking is a valid tool to build bridges and connect design to different disciplines. Design Thinking can also be used as a trojan horse to infiltrate design where nobody would expect it to be.

I'm using Design Thinking as a tactic to fulfil my strategy to have design in conversations about planning and ideating customers facing products and services.

To explain what design is and its value, I have been running several Intro to Design Thinking workshop across several and diverse teams and departments in the business, including HR. My facilitation task has been effortless and fun because of the exceptional audience that actively participated with curiosity, interest, and excitement.

Zopa Design System

There isn't much I can share at the moment (May 2020) as everything is undergoing and the actual outcome is still undefined. However, we are moving fast in delivering a new Zopa by practising our new design principles and developing and scaling our Design System.

To be successfully strategic in design,
listen to the people empathically and understand the business’ model. Act according to the organisation design maturity, cognitive motivations, and business' expectations. Look at the shape and size of the aperture before picking the right peg – or you might end up trying to force a square peg into a round hole. Avoid any cookie-cutter thinking.

Play and Learn

"Play and Learn" is the preservation of my old tradition to spend the last hours of the working week in personal and team development. Friday afternoons are, in most cases, not very productive. So, why don’t use that positive pre-weekend energy to do something fun and constructive? Evert team member has the chance to facilitate and lead an activity; from workshops to Ted style talks, we strive to learn and practice new perspectives and skills. Are these sessions just for designers? Absolutely not, we promote, welcome, and love to have any curious mind joining us.
